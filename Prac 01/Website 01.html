Requirement Gathering & Fact Finding Techniques


What is a Requirement?
  • A statement of what the system must do,

  • A statement of characteristics the system must have,

What is mean by Fact Finding?

  • To study any system the analyst needs to do collect facts and all relevant information.

  • The facts when expressed in quantitative form are termed as data.

  • The success of any project is depended upon the accuracy of available data.

  • Accurate information can be collected with help of certain methods/ techniques.

  • These specific methods for finding information of the system are termed as fact finding techniques.
Fact Finding Techniques : 01) Interviews

This is the most commonly used technique.

 02) Quesionnaires

This is one of the fastest fact collecting technique.

 03) Observations

This method allow us to verify our collected facts.

 04) Social Media

Nowadays, using social media is the fastest method to collect data.

 05) Record Views

The information related to the system is published in the sources like newspapers, magazines, journals, documents etc.
Important facts about fact finding...
Steps to carry out an interview.
  1. Selecting Interviewees.

  2. Designing Interview Questions.

  3. Preparing for the Interview.

  4. Conducting the Interview.

  5. Post-Interview Follow-up.
There are three main types of questions.
  1. Open ended questions.

    • What are some problems you face on a daily basis?

    • What are the improvements of your business?

  2. Close ended questions.

    • How many customer place orders for a day?

    • How many employees do you have?

  3. Probing questions.

    • Why?

    • Can you give me an example?

    • Can you show us the reports?
Advantages of Fact Finding Techniques.
  1. Interviews.

    • Can collect detailed information.
    • It allows exploration and follow-up questions. .
    • Interviews build rapport between the users and the systems analyst.

  2. Quesionnaires.

    • Need low expenses.
    • Can gain results quickly.
    • Can perform at many people at once.

  3. Observations.

    • Allows the validity of facts and data to be checked.
    • No need of much equipment and tools.
    • Easy to complete.
    • Time Saving

  4. Record Views.

    • Records are available any time.
    • Less expensive when compared to interviews.
    • Data security is better.
 Disadvantages of Fact Finding Techniques.
  1. Interviews.

    • Can't perform at many people at a time.
    • May not meet the exact person we want.
    • More time consuming.

  2. Quesionnaires.

    • People may provide dishonest answers.
    • Provide irrelevant answers.

  3. Observations.

    • Human limitations.
    • May require training.
    • Potential to overlook meaningful aspects.

  4. Record Views.

    • This method isn't transparent for the users.
    • May have errors in collected data.
